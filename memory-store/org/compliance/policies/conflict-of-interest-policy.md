# Conflict of Interest Policy

**Owner:** Legal  
**Effective:** 2023-03-01  
**Last Updated:** 2024-06-10

---

A conflict of interest arises when personal interests could — or could appear to — influence professional decisions. This policy defines what counts as a conflict, how to disclose it, and how we manage it.

## Examples of Conflicts

- Owning more than 1% equity in a competitor or potential vendor
- Hiring a family member into a role you influence
- Receiving consulting income from a company that is a vendor candidate
- Sitting on the board of an organization that does business with us
- Directing business to a company in which you have a financial interest

*These are illustrative, not exhaustive.*

## Disclosure Requirements

- New employees must complete a conflicts disclosure within their first 30 days.
- All employees must recertify annually in Workday.
- Any new conflict must be disclosed within **10 business days** of it arising.
- Disclosures go to `legal@company.internal` and are reviewed by the General Counsel.

## Review and Management

Legal will assess each disclosure and determine one of:

1. **No conflict** — disclosed relationship does not create material risk.
2. **Manageable conflict** — employee is recused from specific decisions. Recusal is documented in the conflict register.
3. **Unresolvable conflict** — employee must divest, resign from the outside role, or in rare cases separate from the company.

Decisions are communicated in writing within 15 business days of a complete disclosure.

## Confidentiality

Disclosures are treated as confidential and shared only with those who need to know to manage the conflict.

## Violations

Failing to disclose a known conflict is a Code of Conduct violation and may result in termination. See [Code of Conduct](code-of-conduct.md).
