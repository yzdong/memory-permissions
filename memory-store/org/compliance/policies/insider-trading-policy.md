# Insider Trading Policy

**Owner:** Legal (Securities Counsel)  
**Effective Date:** 2022-07-01  
**Last Updated:** 2024-01-10  
**Applies To:** All employees, directors, contractors, and their immediate family members

---

## Overview

As a company with access to material non-public information (MNPI), all of us have a legal obligation not to trade securities — ours or those of public companies we interact with — based on that information. Violations can result in criminal prosecution, civil penalties, and serious reputational harm. This is an area where "I didn't know" is not a defense.

## What Is MNPI?

Material non-public information is any information that:

1. **Is material:** A reasonable investor would consider it important when deciding to buy or sell securities. Examples:
   - Unannounced earnings or significant changes in forecast
   - Pending mergers, acquisitions, or divestitures
   - Major new customer wins or contract losses
   - Regulatory approvals or rejections
   - Significant cybersecurity incidents

2. **Is non-public:** Not yet released through official public channels (press release, SEC filing, etc.)

## Prohibited Conduct

- Trading company securities while in possession of MNPI
- Tipping MNPI to others — even if you don't trade yourself, passing the information along is illegal
- Trading securities of other companies (partners, customers, acquisition targets) based on MNPI about them

## Trading Windows

Designated insiders (executives, directors, and select employees) may only trade during open trading windows, which open 2 business days after quarterly earnings are released and close at the end of the third month of each fiscal quarter.

If you're unsure whether you're a designated insider, ask Legal.

## Pre-Clearance

All designated insiders must obtain pre-clearance from Legal before executing any trade in company securities. Submit the pre-clearance request form at least 3 business days before the intended trade date.

## 10b5-1 Plans

Designated insiders may establish pre-planned trading plans under Rule 10b5-1. These plans allow trading even outside open windows if properly structured. Legal must review and approve all 10b5-1 plans before they are executed.

## Reporting Concerns

If you suspect someone is trading on MNPI, report it confidentially to Legal or via the ethics hotline at `ethics.company.internal`. Do not confront the individual directly.

## Consequences

Violations may result in immediate termination, disgorgement of profits, civil penalties, and criminal prosecution by the SEC or DOJ. We will cooperate fully with any government investigation.

## Annual Acknowledgment

All employees must acknowledge this policy annually during compliance certification. HR tracks completion.

## Questions

Contact `legal@company.internal` or the Securities Counsel directly. When in doubt, don't trade and ask first.
