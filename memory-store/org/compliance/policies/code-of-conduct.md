# Code of Conduct

**Version:** 2.3  
**Effective:** 2024-02-01  
**Owner:** People & Culture, Legal

---

## Our Commitments

We're building something we're proud of, and how we do it matters as much as what we build. This Code of Conduct describes the standards of behavior we hold ourselves to — not as a legal formality, but as a genuine commitment to each other and to the people we serve.

## Who This Applies To

Everyone who works here: employees, contractors, consultants, board members, and anyone else acting on behalf of the company — in offices, remote, at conferences, or anywhere the company's name is attached.

## Core Principles

### Integrity

- Be honest in all business dealings. Don't misrepresent facts to customers, partners, regulators, or colleagues.
- Disclose conflicts of interest proactively. Use the form at `../hr/conflict-of-interest-disclosure.md`.
- Never falsify records, reports, or expense claims.

### Respect

- Treat everyone with dignity regardless of role, background, identity, or seniority.
- Harassment of any kind — verbal, written, physical, or digital — is not tolerated.
- Constructive disagreement is welcome; personal attacks are not.

### Confidentiality

- Protect proprietary company information and customer data.
- Don't discuss confidential matters in public spaces or on unsecured channels.
- Employees with access to material non-public information must follow the Insider Trading Policy.

### Compliance with Laws

- Follow all applicable laws and regulations wherever you operate.
- When in doubt, ask Legal before acting. It's always easier to prevent a problem than to fix one.

## Conflicts of Interest

A conflict of interest arises when personal interests could influence — or appear to influence — your professional judgment. Common situations include:

- Significant financial interest in a vendor, customer, or competitor
- Outside employment that competes with our business
- Personal relationships that affect hiring, contracting, or performance decisions

Disclose potential conflicts to your manager and HR. We'd rather surface and manage them than have them fester.

## Reporting Concerns

If you see something that doesn't sit right:

- Talk to your manager or HR
- Contact Legal directly
- Use the anonymous ethics hotline: `ethics.company.internal`

We do not retaliate against good-faith reporters. Period.

## Disciplinary Action

Violations are taken seriously. Outcomes range from coaching to termination depending on severity. Conduct that violates the law may be referred to relevant authorities.
