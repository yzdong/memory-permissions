# Intercompany Transfer Pricing Policy

**Owner:** Tax & Treasury  
**Classification:** Confidential — Finance Internal  
**Last Reviewed:** November 2025  
**Audience:** Finance, Legal, and Senior Business Leadership only

## Overview

Transfer pricing governs how we price transactions between our legal entities. This matters for tax compliance (arm's-length standard under OECD guidelines) and for internal P&L accuracy. Non-compliance can result in penalties, double taxation, and audit scrutiny from multiple jurisdictions.

## In-Scope Transactions

- Intercompany services (shared services, management fees, IP licensing)
- Goods transferred between manufacturing/distribution entities
- Intercompany loans and cash pooling arrangements
- Cost-sharing arrangements for R&D

## Arm's-Length Pricing Methods

We use the following methods, in order of preference based on available comparables:

1. **Comparable Uncontrolled Price (CUP)** — when third-party pricing data exists.
2. **Cost Plus** — for service entities where margins can be benchmarked.
3. **Transactional Net Margin Method (TNMM)** — default for most intercompany service arrangements.
4. **Profit Split** — reserved for unique or high-value intangibles where other methods are not reliable.

Tax selects the method; business units do not choose their preferred method.

## Markup Rates (Current)

Service markups are reviewed annually by Tax and updated in the intercompany rate schedule (`teams/finance/resources/ic-rate-schedule-2026.xlsx`). Using stale rates is a compliance risk — always pull from the current schedule.

## Intercompany Agreements

Every intercompany service arrangement must be documented in a written intercompany agreement signed before services begin. Legal maintains these in ContractWorks. If you're initiating a new intercompany flow, notify Tax at least **6 weeks in advance** — retroactive documentation is a red flag for tax authorities.

## Billing Cycle

- Intercompany invoices are issued monthly by the 5th of the following month.
- Settlement occurs via intercompany clearing accounts in NetSuite, not cash wire (except for entities in cash-pooling arrangements).
- Reconciliation of intercompany balances is performed monthly. Unreconciled differences over **$10,000** are escalated to the Controller within 5 business days of month close.

## Country-Specific Considerations

- **Germany / EU:** Subject to EU Anti-Tax Avoidance Directive (ATAD). Legal must review any new IC arrangement involving EU entities.
- **India:** Withholding tax applies to certain services. Tax team coordinates with local advisors.
- **UK:** Post-Brexit rules apply; CbCR filing required if UK entity exceeds threshold revenue.

## Transfer Pricing Documentation

We maintain a Master File and Local Files for each material jurisdiction. These are updated annually before the statutory deadline. The Master File is owned by Tax; Local Files are prepared with support from local accounting teams.

Do not share transfer pricing documentation externally without explicit sign-off from Tax and Legal.

## Questions

Contact `tax@company.internal`. Do not make ad-hoc decisions about intercompany pricing — the downstream compliance implications are significant.
