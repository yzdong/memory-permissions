# Reimbursement Cutoffs & Late Submission Policy

**Owner:** Finance Operations  
**Last Updated:** 2025-09-18

---

## Standard Submission Window

Expenses must be submitted within **45 calendar days** of the date of purchase or last day of travel, whichever is later. This is a hard cutoff aligned with our monthly close schedule.

## What Happens After 45 Days

- Submissions received between **46–90 days** after incurrence require **VP approval** to process. Finance will not chase approvals on the employee's behalf.
- Submissions received **after 90 days** are denied by default. Exceptions require CFO sign-off and are granted rarely (e.g., medical emergency, bereavement).

This policy is not negotiable for year-end close. The absolute hard stop for prior-year expenses is **January 10** of the following year.

## Year-End Schedule

| Deadline | What's Affected |
|---|---|
| Dec 20 | All Q4 expenses must be submitted |
| Dec 27 | Final manager approvals in Expensify |
| Jan 5 | Finance review complete |
| Jan 10 | Absolute cutoff — no prior-year reimbursements after this date |

Finance will send reminders at **T-14**, **T-7**, and **T-2** days before each deadline.

## Handling Disputed Amounts

If a reimbursement is partially denied:

1. Employee receives an automated email from Expensify with line-item notes.
2. Employee may dispute within **10 business days** by replying to `finance-ops@company.internal`.
3. Finance will respond within 5 business days. If unresolved, escalates to Finance VP.

## Why This Exists

Late submissions create accrual errors that cascade into reporting inaccuracies. The 45-day window gives us enough lead time to close monthly books cleanly. If this is consistently painful for your team, raise it in the next Finance Q-review — we're open to workflow changes.
