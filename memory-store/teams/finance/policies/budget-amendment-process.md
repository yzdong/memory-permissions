# Budget Amendment Process

**Owner:** Finance Business Partners  
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-01  
**Applies To:** All department heads managing allocated headcount or OpEx budgets

---

## When Is an Amendment Required?

You need a formal budget amendment if you want to:

- Reallocate more than **$10,000** between cost categories (e.g., headcount to software)
- Exceed your approved annual budget for any single category by more than **7%**
- Add net-new spend not included in the original annual plan

Small intra-category reallocs below $10K can be handled informally — just notify your Finance BP in `#finance-bps` so they can track it.

## Amendment Types

### Type A — Reallocation (no net new spend)
Moving budget between approved line items within the same department. Fastest to approve.

### Type B — In-Year Addition (net new spend)
Requesting budget beyond what was originally approved. Requires business case and competes with other in-year requests.

## Submission Steps

1. Download the **Budget Amendment Request** template from the Finance Confluence space.
2. Complete sections: current budget, requested change, business justification, expected ROI or cost avoidance rationale.
3. Submit to your Finance BP at least **10 business days before** the spend is needed.
4. Finance BP reviews and forwards to Finance VP (for Type A) or CFO (for Type B) with a recommendation.
5. Decision communicated within 5 business days of Finance VP/CFO receipt.

## Approval Authority

| Amendment Type | Amount | Approver |
|---|---|---|
| Type A | Any | Finance VP |
| Type B | < $100K | Finance VP |
| Type B | $100K – $500K | CFO |
| Type B | > $500K | CFO + Board Finance Committee |

## Tips From the Finance Team

- Submit Type B requests at least 3 weeks out. We've had requests fail not because they were bad ideas but because they arrived too late to process before the spend date.
- Include a named cost center even in the draft — it speeds up processing significantly.
- If you're not sure whether you need an amendment, ask your Finance BP first. It's almost always faster to ask than to find out after the fact.
