# Budget Variance Reporting Policy

**Owner:** FP&A  
**Classification:** Confidential  
**Cycle:** Monthly, with quarterly deep-dives

## Purpose

Variance reporting keeps budget holders honest and gives Finance visibility to reforecast accurately. This document defines thresholds, timelines, and what we expect from department leads when variances occur.

## Reporting Cadence

- **Monthly Flash:** Distributed by FP&A on the 8th business day after month close. Department heads receive their own cost center view.
- **Quarterly Business Review (QBR) Pack:** Distributed 3 business days before each QBR. Includes full-year forecast, actuals-to-date, and variance commentary.
- **Ad-hoc requests:** FP&A may request a variance explanation within 48 hours when a line item flags in our anomaly detection model.

## Variance Thresholds Requiring Written Commentary

| Category | Threshold (vs. Budget) |
|----------|------------------------|
| Headcount costs | >5% or >$50,000, whichever is lower |
| Vendor / external spend | >10% or >$30,000 |
| Travel & entertainment | >15% |
| Capital expenditure | >8% |

Commentary is due in NetSuite within **3 business days** of the flash report distribution. FP&A will follow up directly with department leads who miss this deadline.

## What Good Commentary Looks Like

Bad: *"Spend was higher than budget due to business needs."*  
Good: *"Engineering headcount variance of $42K reflects two backfills that were approved mid-quarter after the budget freeze was lifted (approved by VP Eng on Oct 14). No further hiring is planned this quarter."*

Be specific. Reference approval dates or emails if relevant.

## Forecast Adjustments

Department heads may submit a reforecast request to FP&A once per quarter (outside of the standard QBR cycle) if a material event has occurred — new contract, headcount change, unexpected one-time cost. Use the form at `teams/finance/templates/reforecast-request.md`.

FP&A will update the model within 5 business days and notify Finance leadership.

## Escalation

If a department is running more than **20% over budget** on a given line and no variance explanation has been provided within 5 business days, FP&A escalates to the CFO. This is not punitive — it's a signal that we need to understand what's happening before it compounds.

## Data Source

All variance reports are sourced from NetSuite actuals. If you believe there's an error in the underlying data, contact AP or the Controller's team — do not dispute the variance report itself until the source data is confirmed.
