# Vendor Insurance Requirements

**Type:** Internal Policy Reference  
**Owner:** Finance (Marcus Webb) + Legal  
**Last Reviewed:** 2025-10-01  
**Next Review:** 2026-10-01

## Purpose

This document defines the minimum insurance requirements Finance enforces for all vendor contracts. Legal reviews are required for any deviations. Procurement must not execute agreements where vendors don't meet these minimums without written CFO and GC approval.

## Standard Minimums by Vendor Category

### SaaS / Software Vendors

| Coverage Type | Minimum Limit |
|--------------|---------------|
| Commercial General Liability | $2,000,000 per occurrence / $4,000,000 aggregate |
| Errors & Omissions (Tech E&O) | $5,000,000 per claim |
| Cyber Liability | $5,000,000 per claim |
| Workers' Compensation | Statutory limits |
| Employer's Liability | $1,000,000 per occurrence |

### Professional Services / Consultants

| Coverage Type | Minimum Limit |
|--------------|---------------|
| Commercial General Liability | $1,000,000 per occurrence |
| Professional Liability (E&O) | $2,000,000 per claim |
| Cyber Liability | $2,000,000 (if handling our data) |
| Workers' Compensation | Statutory limits |

### Real Estate / Facilities Vendors

| Coverage Type | Minimum Limit |
|--------------|---------------|
| Commercial General Liability | $3,000,000 per occurrence |
| Property (contractor's equipment) | Replacement cost |
| Workers' Compensation | Statutory limits |

## Process

1. Procurement requests certificate of insurance (COI) before contract execution
2. COI must name our company as Additional Insured on GL and Auto policies
3. Vendors must notify us within 10 days of material policy changes or cancellation
4. COIs stored in ContractSafe under each vendor's contract record
5. Annual COI refresh required for multi-year agreements

## Common Issues

- Startups often carry lower cyber limits — flag to Legal; sometimes acceptable with risk acceptance doc
- International vendors may carry policies in non-USD — convert at contract date exchange rate
- Outside counsel firms: covered separately under their professional liability; see `outside-counsel-panel-agreement.md`
