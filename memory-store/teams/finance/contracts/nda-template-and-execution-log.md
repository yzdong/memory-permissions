# NDA Template & Execution Log

**Owner:** Legal (GC office) with Finance procurement support  
**Last Updated:** 2025-12-01

## Overview

This file describes our standard NDA approach for vendor and partner relationships, and tracks significant NDAs executed as part of procurement or contract negotiations. Full signed documents are in ContractSafe under the relevant vendor record.

## Our Standard NDA

We use a mutual NDA template maintained by Legal. Current version: v4.2 (updated September 2025 to address AI/LLM use of confidential information).

Template location: `../../../legal/templates/mutual-nda-v4.2.docx`  
Do not use any prior version without Legal sign-off — v4.1 and earlier lack the AI data use clause.

### Key Provisions

- **Term:** 3 years from execution, confidentiality obligations survive 2 years post-term
- **Definition of Confidential Information:** Broad; includes oral disclosures if confirmed in writing within 10 days
- **Exclusions:** Standard (publicly known, independently developed, received from third party without restriction)
- **AI/ML clause (added v4.2):** Receiving party may not use disclosed information to train or fine-tune AI models without prior written consent
- **Governing law:** Delaware, with exclusive jurisdiction in Delaware courts
- **Return/destroy:** 30 days written request; we prefer destroy with certification

## Vendor Redlines We Commonly See

- Many vendors push for their own paper. We accept vendor paper only for large enterprise vendors (AWS, Salesforce, etc.) where pushing our template is impractical. Log this as an exception.
- Common redline: extending residual knowledge carve-out. We narrow this language; Legal has approved fall-back position on file.
- Jurisdiction changes: vendors sometimes push for California or New York. We accept California; push back on NY and foreign jurisdictions.

## Execution Process

1. Requestor submits NDA request via Legal intake form
2. Legal reviews counterparty's paper or sends our template
3. Execution via DocuSign (Finance ops has admin access)
4. Executed copy uploaded to ContractSafe and linked to vendor record
5. Expiry date entered into contract calendar for renewal/archival
